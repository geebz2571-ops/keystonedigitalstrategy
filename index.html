Why Now

Payment technology is evolving faster than at any point in decades. Evaluating whether it can deliver better, cheaper, more reliable service to residents isn't chasing hype — it's basic fiduciary stewardship. Standing still is itself a decision, and often an expensive one.

Stablecoins are one example of how far the frontier has moved: with the federal GENIUS Act signed into law in July 2025, states now have a national standard, and the question has shifted from "why would we" to "how do we evaluate this responsibly." The same is true across instant rails, real-time payments, and programmable disbursement.

There are incremental payment upgrades, and there are structural ones. The pace of change rewards offices that explore early and design carefully.

Early movers

Wyoming

Issued FRNT, the first U.S. state-issued digital dollar, fully reserved and managed by Franklin Templeton. Interest on the reserves funds the state’s School Foundation Program.

Why it matters: early proof a state can capture new value from how it holds and moves funds — without a new tax.

Nebraska

Telcoin, chartered as the first U.S. digital-asset bank, issued eUSD — the country’s first bank-issued, fully-reserved dollar stablecoin (live since December 2025).

Why it matters: a state used its own chartering authority to bring a regulated issuer onshore — keeping the institution and its oversight in-state.

These are early examples, not endorsements of any one path. Our role is to help you weigh the options on the merits — cost, speed, control, and risk.

Fluent in the standards your programs answer to — public procurement and cooperative purchasing, GASB, Treasury and banking regulation, and emerging digital-asset policy, including the federal GENIUS Act.

Frequently Asked

Do you only work on stablecoins?
No — we're technology-agnostic. Most engagements are about reducing cost and friction in the rails you already run: cards, ACH, disbursement, and vendor contracts. Where newer options like stablecoins or real-time payments genuinely help, we'll show you the math and the risks — and where they don't, we'll say so.
How do you stay objective?
Our analysis starts from your goals and your numbers, and we lay out the alternatives — so the recommendation is one you can defend. Where a specific solution fits, we'll show you why, alongside the options you'd be choosing between.
Is this about crypto?
We focus on regulated, fully-reserved instruments and proven rails — evaluated on cost, speed, control, and risk like any other option. We're not in the business of speculative assets.
How are you compensated?
Transparent and fee-based, with scope and fees agreed up front. You always know exactly what you're paying for.
Do you understand public-sector procurement?
Yes. We work within RFP and cooperative-purchasing processes and the fiduciary and GASB constraints your office operates under.
Do you only advise, or also implement?
Both. We stay through implementation, working alongside your team — not handing over a deck and walking away.
